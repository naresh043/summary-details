CHE GUEVERA

About

Ernesto "Che" Guevara was an Argentine Marxist revolutionary, physician, author, guerrilla leader, diplomat, and military theorist. He was a major figure in the Cuban Revolution and his stylized visage has become a ubiquitous countercultural symbol of rebellion and global insignia in popular culture. Born on June 14, 1928, in Rosario, Argentina, Che traveled extensively through South America in his youth, a journey that awakened him to the inequality and exploitation prevalent across the continent. This experience was pivotal in shaping his revolutionary ideologies. After meeting Fidel Castro and his brother Raúl in Mexico, Che joined their 26th of July Movement and played a key role in the guerrilla war against Cuban dictator Fulgencio Batista. Che's efforts were instrumental in the eventual overthrow of Batista in 1959, after which he held several key positions in the new government. Che left Cuba in the mid-1960s to foster revolution internationally, first in Congo-Kinshasa and later in Bolivia, where he was captured by CIA-assisted Bolivian forces and executed on October 9, 1967. Che Guevara remains a controversial and complex figure, revered by some as a hero who fought for the oppressed, and criticized by others as a ruthless executioner. His written works, including "The Motorcycle Diaries," which details his early travels, and "Guerrilla Warfare," provide insight into his thoughts and methods.

Rajini kanth

About

Rajinikanth, born Shivaji Rao Gaekwad, has a truly inspirational story marked by his rise from humble beginnings to becoming a superstar in Indian cinema. His journey began as a bus conductor working in Bangalore, where his charisma and flair were already evident, entertaining passengers with his style and mannerisms. Driven by his passion for acting, Rajinikanth joined the Madras Film Institute to pursue a career in films. His big break came with the film "Apoorva Raagangal" (1975), directed by K. Balachander, who also renamed him Rajinikanth. His unique style and powerful screen presence quickly made him a favorite among audiences. Rajinikanth’s rise to stardom is not just a testament to his acting skills but also his profound personal qualities: humility, spiritual seeking, and a never-give-up attitude. Despite facing numerous challenges and setbacks, his perseverance led him to become an icon of Indian cinema, admired not only for his film roles but also for his off-screen simplicity and philanthropy. His life story is a powerful reminder that with determination and hard work, anyone can overcome obstacles and achieve great success.

Pawan kalyan

About

Konidela Pawan Kalyan (born Konidela Kalyan Babu; 2 September 1968 or 1971[n 1]) is an Indian actor, politician, filmmaker, martial artist, and philanthropist who works in Telugu cinema. Known for his unique acting style and mannerisms, he has a large fan base and a cult following. One of the highest-paid actors of Indian cinema, he has been featured in Forbes India's Celebrity 100 list since 2013 and is the recipient of a Filmfare Award, a SIIMA Award, a CineMAA Award, and a Santosham Film Award.[5] He is also the founder of the Jana Sena Party. Kalyan made his acting debut in the 1996 film Akkada Ammayi Ikkada Abbayi but rose to prominence with the dramas Gokulamlo Seeta (1997) and Suswagatham (1998). He achieved stardom for his performance in Tholi Prema (1998), which won the National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Telugu that year. Kalyan established himself as a leading actor with successful projects such as Thammudu (1999), Badri (2000), Kushi (2001), Balu (2005), Jalsa (2008), Gabbar Singh (2012), Attarintiki Daredi (2013), Gopala Gopala (2015), Vakeel Saab (2021), and Bheemla Nayak (2022).[6] He received the Filmfare Award for Best Actor – Telugu for Gabbar Singh while Attarintiki Daredi held the record of being the highest-grossing Telugu film of all time.[7][8] In addition to acting, he produces films under the banners Anjana Productions and Pawan Kalyan Creative Works.[9] In 2008, Kalyan entered into politics as the youth wing president of his brother Chiranjeevi's Praja Rajyam Party, but he left after it merged into the Congress party. He founded the Jana Sena Party in March 2014 and[10][11] was listed as the most searched Indian celebrity politician on Google at the time.[12] Kalyan is a known philanthropist and is the founder of the Andhra Pradesh charity [16]Common Man Protection Force.[17][18] He is a black belt in Karate and trains in various martial arts which he depicts in his films regularly. Kalyan is referred to as Power Star by his fans and in the media.
